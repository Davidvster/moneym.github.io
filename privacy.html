Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 12, 2026

1. Overview. This Privacy Policy explains, in general terms, how the MoneyM Application handles information when you use it. The Service is designed around local‑first storage and does not rely on a central backend controlled by the developer.

2. Information stored on your device. The Service stores the transactions, categories, notes, settings and display preferences you enter directly on your device. This information stays on your device unless you actively choose to export or back it up. If you uninstall the Application or clear its data in your device settings, this information will normally be removed from that device.

3. Optional Google Drive backup. If you enable the backup feature, the Service will request permission to access your Google account and Google Drive so that it can create and update backup files in the Service's private application folder within your Drive. Those backup files may contain the same information you have in the Application (for example, transactions and categories) and exist solely so that you can restore your data on the same or another device. The developer does not operate a separate server that stores your personal budgeting data, and does not have direct access to your Google account or Google Drive contents.

4. Optional bank synchronization (Enable Banking). If you enable the optional bank sync feature, the Application connects directly from your device to the Enable Banking API using the Enable Banking application credentials you registered yourself. Imported transaction data is delivered straight from Enable Banking to your device and stored locally like any other data you enter. Your Enable Banking application ID and private key are stored only in your device's secure keystore. The developer operates no servers: none of your banking data, credentials or other information is transmitted to, stored on, or accessible by the developer or any MoneyM server. Enable Banking's own processing of your data is described in Enable Banking's privacy policy, which applies independently of this one.

5. Other permissions. Depending on your platform, the Application may request system‑level permissions such as network access (for Google Drive backup) or biometric authentication (for unlocking the Application). These permissions are used only to provide the corresponding feature and not to collect additional categories of personal information.

6. No advertising or third‑party tracking SDK. The Application does not include built‑in advertising libraries or third‑party analytics SDKs intended to track your behavior across apps or services.

7. Children. The Service is intended for adults and is not directed to children under the age of 13. The Application does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe that a child has used the Service and provided information on a device you control, you should delete the data from that device and remove any associated backups (for example, from Google Drive).

8. Your rights and local laws. Depending on where you live, you may have certain rights under data‑protection or privacy laws (for example, rights of access, correction or deletion under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or rights under California privacy laws such as the CCPA/CPRA). Because the Service is local‑first and does not operate its own user account system or central database, most of these rights can be exercised directly by you on your device (for example, by viewing, editing or deleting data in the Application, uninstalling the Application, or deleting backup files from your Google Drive account). This document does not attempt to reproduce full GDPR, CCPA/CPRA or CalOPPA wording and is not legal advice; if those laws apply to you, you should review your obligations separately and adapt this Policy with the help of a qualified professional.

9. Retention and deletion. You control how long your budgeting data is kept. You can delete individual entries in the Application, reset all data from within the Application if that option is provided, uninstall the Application, or manually delete backup files from your Google Drive account. The developer does not keep an independent server‑side copy of this information.

10. Changes to this Policy. We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the Service or in applicable requirements. The most current version will be made available from within the Application or on the project page. Your continued use of the Service after an updated Policy becomes effective will mean that you accept the updated Policy.

11. Contact. If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, you can contact us through the project page at https://github.com/Davidvster/moneym.
